My Long Term Life Goals

Scroll down to learn more about four of my most important life goals.

1. Full-Time Career Working with Human & Computer Languages:

Whether it's human or computer, languages of all kinds fascinate me. To have a career devoted to learning vast depths of languages, the anthropological study of language, and the psychology of language in all living beings on earth is one of my greatest life goals.

2. Raise Children & Spend More Time in Nature:

It's no secret in our current society that being able to raise children, being able to spend ample time away from money-making ventures to be able to spend time doing hobbies is a privilege. Where I currently live, in California, this is especially true. With cost of living so high, raising children and spending time doing hobbies has become a luxury. However, this goal of mine is still one I am pursuing earnestly. Being adopted, I have always wanted to adopt. I also want to spend more time in nature, which is one of my main hobbies and also a part of my spirituality faith.

3. Learn the Following Languages to at least a B2 Level:

Mandarin, American Sign Language, Spanish, Russian, Arabic, Irish Gaelic and Jamaican Patois.

4. Complete My Art Projects and Publish Them.

I am naturally a more reserved person. I do not like being the center of attention or being on camera. These qualities do not lend themselves well to the heart of someone who also desires to create and share their art. Art of all kinds can be largely divisive, evoking and stimulating in ways that can garner unwanted attention to the artist. This has kept me from releasing and sharing any of my art besides my photography. My goal is to get to the point where I am able to release all of my art and experience in the joy of others' experiencing something I have created.
